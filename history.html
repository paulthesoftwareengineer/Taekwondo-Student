The History of Taekwondo

Taekwondo is a traditional Korean martial art that combines combat techniques, self-defense, sport, exercise, meditation, and philosophy. Its history dates back over 2,000 years.

Origins

In ancient Korea, there were three primary kingdoms: Goguryeo, Baekje, and Silla. Each of these had their own martial arts systems, which laid the foundation for what would eventually become Taekwondo.

Modern Evolution

Modern Taekwondo began to take shape in the 1940s and 1950s when Korean martial artists who had studied in Japan during its occupation returned to Korea. Over the next few decades, these martial arts schools, or "kwans," began to unify under a single system and philosophy, resulting in the birth of Taekwondo as we know it today.

Global Recognition

Over the years, Taekwondo gained international recognition, especially after its introduction to the Olympics. Today, it's practiced by millions worldwide, not just as a martial art, but as a way of life.
