Taekwondo Belts and Their Significance

There are six belts in Taekwondo: white, yellow, green, blue, red, and black. Each belt represents a level of skill and accomplishment. The student must demonstrate proficiency in the skills required for each belt before advancing to the next level.

White Belt

White represents purity and innocence. It signifies the beginning of a journey where the student is a blank slate, eager to learn.

Requirements: Basic stances, introductory punches, and kicks.

Black Belt

Black symbolizes the darkness beyond the Sun. A black belt seeks new, deeper knowledge of the art.

Requirements: Mastery of all forms, advanced sparring techniques, understanding of Taekwondo's philosophy, and teaching ability.
